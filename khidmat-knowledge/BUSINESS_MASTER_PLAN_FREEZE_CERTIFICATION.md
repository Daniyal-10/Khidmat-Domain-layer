## What Remains Intentionally Deferred

The following are not defects and are not blocking this certification. They are deliberately unresolved, tracked in `BUSINESS_MASTER_PLAN_RESEARCH_BACKLOG.md`, and constitute the BMP's honestly-declared boundary of current knowledge:

- **The programme-validated intervention catalogue** (Backlog RB-C1) — the single largest, previously and independently flagged gap, requiring programme-staff input the BMP correctly did not attempt to invent.
- **Religiously-obligated giving's specific mechanics** (Backlog RB-B1–B3) — acknowledged as a distinct business reality throughout the BMP, never described in operative detail, by design.
- **All priority orderings, eligibility rules, decision-rights assignments, and actor-to-capability assignments** (Backlog Workstream D) — correctly held for Business Architecture, not the BMP.
- **All process-trigger mechanisms** — what specifically marks a stage transition, a reassessment, a value-stream shift, or a governance escalation (Backlog Workstream E).
- **Domain-expert validation of exhaustiveness claims** across actors, tensions, capabilities, value streams, intervention categories, objectives, and variation dimensions (Backlog Workstream A) — none of the BMP's enumerations claim completeness, and none should be read as claiming it now.
- **A small set of gaps newly identified during review** — anticipatory/predictive need detection, consent as a named business concept, voluntary withdrawal, inaccurate/fraudulent claims, an untested equity-variation question in Chapter 9, and internal terminology reconciliation ("capacity," "resilience") (Backlog Workstream F).

None of these items contradict any statement currently in the BMP. All are additions or clarifications the BMP itself, correctly, did not attempt to originate without proper input.

## What Downstream Documents May Now Assume

Authors of the Humanitarian Business Reference Model, Business Architecture, and Domain Discovery may treat the following as settled, frozen foundation:

- The **actor categories** in Chapter 1 and the **business function** each performs.
- The **stakeholder interests and named tensions** in Chapter 2, as legitimate and unresolved by design — not as a menu to be quietly settled by whichever document happens to address them next.
- The **ten-stage business lifecycle narrative** in Chapter 3, understood as non-linear, non-sequential in practice, and not a workflow specification.
- The **thirteen business capabilities** in Chapter 4, as standing abilities independent of any organizational assignment.
- The **business-service and value-stream framing** in Chapter 5, including the five representative value-stream patterns as illustrative, not exhaustive or mandatory.
- The **eight intervention categories** in Chapter 6, as business-intent groupings that combine rather than compete, with no eligibility content attached.
- The **ten operational objectives and four named trade-offs** in Chapter 7, with no priority ordering implied among any of them.
- The **seven governance authority boundaries** in Chapter 8, and the knowledge-based rationale for why authority is distributed rather than centralized.
- The **universal/variable discipline** in Chapter 9 (§9.1) as a reusable method, and the eight dimensions of variation as illustrative applications of it.
- The relationship, per `BUSINESS_MASTER_PLAN_RESOLUTION.md`, between this document and `KHIDMAT_BUSINESS_LOGIC_BLUEPRINT_V1.md`: the BMP is the timeless business-domain description; the V1 Blueprint is the versioned, implementation-anchored specification of what the current system delivers against that domain. Neither supersedes the other.

## What Downstream Documents Must Not Assume

- **That any enumeration in the BMP is exhaustive.** Every numbered list of actors, tensions, capabilities, value streams, categories, objectives, or variation dimensions is explicitly non-exhaustive, per each chapter's own Flagged Open Items and per Backlog Workstream A. A future document may extend any of these lists; it must not treat silence as a claim of completeness.
- **That any named tension, trade-off, or open governance question has been resolved.** Chapters 2, 7, and 8 name tensions and boundaries without ranking or resolving them. A downstream document that must make a prioritization call (e.g., Business Architecture assigning decision rights) is doing new work, not discovering a decision the BMP already made.
- **That the intervention categories in Chapter 6 correspond to any specific, deliverable programme offering.** Backlog RB-C1 remains open and Critical; no downstream document should treat Chapter 6's eight categories as a substitute for the still-missing programme-validated catalogue.
- **That the BMP's silence on anticipatory/predictive need detection means the project does not intend to pursue it.** Per `BUSINESS_MASTER_PLAN_RESOLUTION.md`, `KHIDMAT_BUSINESS_LOGIC_BLUEPRINT_V1.md` already names this as the long-term vision; its absence from the BMP is a registered gap (Backlog RB-F1), not a decision against it.
- **That this certification constitutes business-content review.** This certification, and the two documents it depends on, are governance and traceability actions. They confirm the BMP is internally consistent, blueprint-compliant, and now formally closed for its current draft — they do not constitute domain-expert validation of any specific claim within it. That validation is Backlog Workstream A's job, not this certification's.
