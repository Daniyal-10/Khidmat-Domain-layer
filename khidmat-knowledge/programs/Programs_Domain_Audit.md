# Programs Domain Audit (Deep Maturity Review)

## Executive Summary
This document provides a comprehensive, deep-maturity discovery and audit of the Programs domain. In its initial pass, Programs was understood merely as a "bucket for interventions." After a deep humanitarian review challenging the realities of UN, INGO, and Government welfare operations, the domain's maturity has been significantly expanded to model governance, versions, suspensions, exceptions, and inter-program conflict.

## Business Validation
Humanitarian assistance frequently fails not because the need wasn't assessed, but because the *Program* collapsed. Funding cuts, legal amendments, conflict-zone access loss, donor audits, and programmatic bottlenecks are the realities Program Managers face. The Programs domain validates that Khidmat can model not just the *offerings*, but the structural fragility, regulatory weight, and lifecycle of the initiatives themselves.

## Ownership Analysis
**What Programs Owns (Expanded):**
- **Program Identity & Versioning:** Handling program amendments, renewals, and geographic variations (parent/child programs).
- **Eligibility & Targeting Governance:** Defining criteria, versioning those criteria, and managing prioritization rules for waitlists.
- **Intervention Modality & Conditionality:** Defining what is offered, including milestone-based and phased assistance.
- **Funding & Compliance Constraints:** Modeling earmarked vs unrestricted funds, partner-owned implementation, and strict audit/reporting checkpoints.
- **Program Lifecycle & State:** Managing pilot phases, emergency triggers, suspensions (e.g., due to inflation/conflict), and closures.
- **Cross-Program Relationships:** Defining mutual exclusivity (preventing double-dipping), prerequisite pathways, and successor continuity.
- **Enrollment State & Appeals:** Tracking cohort status, manual humanitarian overrides, and formal appeal pathways for rejection.

## Dependency Analysis
- **Case Management:** Consumes Program eligibility criteria to propose enrollment and draws down on the Intervention Catalogue. Relies on Programs for mutual exclusivity rules.
- **Needs Assessment:** Completely independent. Needs exist objectively; Programs address them selectively.
- **Support Delivery:** Executes interventions defined by Programs.
- **Registration:** Supplies identity and demographic claims evaluated by Eligibility rules.
- **Impact:** Evaluates whether the Program's structural design actually resulted in cohort-level improvement.

## Readiness Assessment
The Programs domain has evolved from a basic catalog to a robust governance layer capable of representing complex humanitarian reality—including government Direct Benefit Transfers, INGO graduation models, and emergency response structures—without hardcoding specific regional schemes. With the inclusion of versioning, overrides, suspensions, and mutual exclusivity, Programs matches the conceptual depth of Registration and Case Management.
