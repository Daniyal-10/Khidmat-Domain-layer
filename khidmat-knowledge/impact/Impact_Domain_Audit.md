# Impact Domain Audit

## Executive Summary
This document provides a comprehensive, deep-maturity discovery and audit of the Impact domain. It evaluates the domain against real-world humanitarian complexity, focusing on longitudinal measurement, outcome indicators, and systemic changes in beneficiary wellbeing over time.

## Business Validation
Impact Measurement represents the ultimate objective of Khidmat AI: understanding whether interventions actually improved human wellbeing. While other domains track inputs (Registration), needs (Needs Assessment), and outputs (Support Delivery), the Impact domain is responsible for measuring actual, sustained changes (baseline vs endline).

## Ownership Analysis
**What Impact Owns:**
- Outcome Indicators: Standardized metrics for measuring changes in health, education, economic stability, etc.
- Longitudinal Assessment: Tracking beneficiary wellbeing over time after assistance has been delivered.
- Impact Evaluation: Determining if an intervention succeeded, failed, or caused unintended consequences.
- Baseline and Endline Comparison: The delta between the initial state and the state post-intervention.

**What Impact Never Owns:**
- Delivery of support (owned by Support Delivery).
- Case closure decisions (owned by Case Management).
- Initial vulnerability and risk scoring (owned by Risk/Needs Assessment).

## Dependency Analysis
- **Case Management**: Provides the context of what interventions were planned and executed, triggering outcome measurement.
- **Needs Assessment**: Provides the baseline data against which impact is measured.
- **Programs**: Requires impact data to evaluate program efficacy and adjust future designs.

## Readiness Assessment
The Impact domain is conceptually acknowledged in the V1 Blueprint but is slated as a future capability. The business vocabulary for outcome-indicator metrics, evaluation methodologies, and longitudinal tracking needs to be rigorously defined to avoid conflating "outputs" (what was given) with "outcomes" (what changed).
