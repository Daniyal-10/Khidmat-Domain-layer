# Case Management Domain Audit

## 1. Domain Purpose
The Case Management domain is responsible for the longitudinal orchestration of care and support. It transforms static needs (from Needs Assessment) into dynamic, accountable action over time. It is the definitive record of *who is doing what, when, and to what end* for a Beneficiary (Individual or Household).

## 2. Boundaries and Ownership

### What Case Management Owns:
- **Case Lifecycle:** The operational state of the case (Open, Active, Suspended, Administratively Closed, Graduated, Reopened).
- **Case Planning:** Interventions, Objectives, Priority, and Sequencing.
- **Coordination & Referrals:** Multi-agency orchestration, case conferences, and referrals (as requests for action).
- **Tracking & Accountability:** Monitoring schedules, follow-ups, case notes, and historical timeline.
- **Ownership & Assignments:** Case workers, lead agencies, statutory vs. operational ownership transfers, caseloads.

### What Case Management Does NOT Own (Strict Boundaries):
- **Beneficiary Identity & Shared Human Model (Shared Domain):** The person exists independently. Case Management merely points to the `Subject`.
- **Needs Assessment & Findings (Needs Assessment / Verification):** Case Management uses assessed needs to build a plan; it does not diagnose or verify.
- **Funding & Programs (Programs Domain):** Grant-linked cases, funding exhaustion, donor restrictions, and program eligibility belong to the Programs domain. Case Management references a Program constraint but does not own funding logic.
- **Support Delivery (Support Delivery Domain):** The physical mechanics of giving aid (logistics, vouchers, cash transfers).
- **Mass/Community Disasters (Community Context / Emergency Response):** In a mass disaster where individualized planning breaks down, the "Community Case" or "Mass Distribution" is handled by Support Delivery directly or Community Context. Case Management activates only when individualized or household-specific longitudinal tracking is required.

## 3. Core Principles
- **Separation of Custody and Operation:** Legal/Statutory ownership is distinct from Operational ownership.
- **Goal-Oriented over Transactional:** Cases exist to achieve Objectives (Graduation), not just to log transactions.
- **Multi-Agency Reality:** A single Household Case may federate responsibilities across multiple external and internal actors, requiring a Lead Coordinator.
- **Resilience to Time:** Cases must survive multi-decade lifespans, funding pauses, temporary abandonment, and agency handoffs without losing coherence.
