# Support Delivery: Unknown Unknowns

This document captures humanitarian operational realities that the current Support Delivery architecture has not yet fully anticipated.

1. **Digital Modality Failures**: What happens when a digital voucher delivery fails because the local telco network is down during the SMS dispatch?
2. **Spontaneous Proxies**: The beneficiary is hospitalized, and an unregistered neighbor attempts to accept the delivery. How does the volunteer dynamically authorize or reject this in the field?
3. **Compound Modalities**: A delivery event that is simultaneously physical (a blanket) and service-oriented (training on how to use a water filter).
4. **Stigmatizing Deliveries**: Handing over a specialized hygiene kit that publicly marks the household as suffering from a specific stigmatized disease. How does the system enforce "blind" packaging?
5. **Deterioration en route**: Cold chain was broken for 2 hours; the goods are physically present but medically void.
6. **Phantom Beneficiaries**: The delivery arrives, but the community reports the beneficiary never existed (fraud detection surface).
