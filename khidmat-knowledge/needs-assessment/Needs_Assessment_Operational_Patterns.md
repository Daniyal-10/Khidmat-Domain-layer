# Needs Assessment Operational Patterns

## 1. Continuous Micro-Assessments (Health Worker Model)
- **Context:** ASHA or Anganwadi workers in India making routine weekly visits.
- **Execution:** Instead of a 100-question PMT, they perform micro-sessions (e.g., a 3-question nutritional check).
- **Pattern:** High-frequency, low-duration Assessment Sessions that incrementally update Evidence Freshness and adjust specific Need Assertions without doing a full baseline.

## 2. Peer Review and Calibration Desks
- **Context:** Large-scale government welfare sweeps where 10,000 enumerators are deployed.
- **Execution:** Supervisors sit at desks reviewing incoming sessions. They run statistical checks to see if an assessor is systematically marking everyone "Critical" (Calibration Issue).
- **Pattern:** Findings remain in `Provisional` status until Supervisor Review clears them. Systemic biases trigger retraining, and those sessions are flagged for Reassessment.

## 3. The Triangulation Review (Multi-Agency)
- **Context:** Refugee camps where UNHCR, WFP, and specialized child protection NGOs all operate.
- **Execution:** WFP conducts food security; UNHCR conducts protection. They overlap on demographic capacities.
- **Pattern:** An automated or manual Finding Consensus engine runs in the background, identifying conflicting observations between agencies and triggering joint reassessments.

## 4. Disaster Rapid Sweeps
- **Context:** Flood response.
- **Execution:** Drones and SDRF teams bypass individual households. They assess entire geographic nodes.
- **Pattern:** Mass Invalidation of old data -> Community-level Assessment Session -> Community-level Need Assertion -> Blanket Support Delivery.
