Executive Summary

Answering one question: is the foundation ready?

The question this review exists to answer: have we implemented the knowledge architecture as originally envisioned, and is the foundation now sufficiently complete to proceed without another major architectural refactor? Every section below contributes evidence toward that answer.

This is not a status update or a whitepaper — it is a validation review. Each architectural decision recorded in the original Business Logic Blueprint and the 28 subsequent Architecture Decision Records is checked here against what actually exists in the repository today, with file-level evidence, so an architectural sign-off can be given with confidence rather than good faith.

Architecturally sound

13 domains, 80 canonical entities, single-ownership discipline intact, zero confirmed broken cross-domain references — independently verified.

Intentionally deferred

Support Interventions, Volunteer Operations Tier 2, Consent & Privacy, and staged reasoning activation are governance decisions, not gaps — see §14.

Requires review before freeze

Two shared sub-modules need canonical migration; Case Management needs its reasoning layer; three governance documents need reconciliation — see §14.

Why the ontology is deep, not just wide

Semantic composition, not flat attributes. A Case does not carry "risk" as a field — it references shared_risk:risk_characterization, which is itself composed from hazard, exposure, vulnerability, and resilience via risk_composition/risk_profile relationships in shared/risk/ontology/risk.yaml.

Lifecycle modeling is a distinct axis. Nine developmental stages (infant → elderly) in the Shared Human Model each carry their own characteristic dependencies, capabilities, and vulnerabilities — a person's age is a developmental context, not an integer.

Value Objects vs. Entities is a deliberate test. ADR-023 defines a four-point promotion test before anything becomes a full Entity — e.g. contact_point and treatment_plan in Registration are structured Value Objects, not entities, because no other entity will ever hold a relationship to one specific instance.

Conclusion

A carefully designed model, faithfully implemented.

Thirteen domains, 80 canonical entities, and roughly 300 controlled-vocabulary terms now share one authoring contract and one governance process — independently verified to have zero confirmed broken cross-domain references. Every conceptual pillar the original Business Logic Blueprint committed to for V1 is implemented, and two domains (Support Delivery, Donor & Resource) now exist beyond that original commitment entirely.

What remains is specific, bounded, and already named: two foundational sub-modules need canonical migration, Case Management needs a reasoning layer, and the pipeline needs one real worked case to prove it end to end. Everything else that is not yet built was deliberately deferred by governance decision, not left incomplete by oversight. None of the remaining work requires re-architecting what exists.
