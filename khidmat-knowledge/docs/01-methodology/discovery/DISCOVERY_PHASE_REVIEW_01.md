# Business Discovery Phase Review — Checkpoint 1

**What this document is:** a retrospective quality review of the Business Discovery *process* across its first three completed topics. It evaluates whether discovery is being conducted rigorously, consistently, and evidence-first — not what humanitarian reality is.
**What this document is not:** it is not a synthesis of TD-01–03's content into narrative form (that would be BMP-shaped work), not a resolution of any open Assumption or Contradiction, not a modification of the Business Discovery Blueprint, and not an authorization to begin the Business Master Plan. This document does not itself clear any gate — the gates already established in TD-01/02/03's own Handoff Records (Human Owner sign-off on CL-001/CL-002) remain exactly as they were before this review.

---

## 1. Coverage

TD-01, TD-02, and TD-03 map exactly to BMP Blueprint Chapters 1, 2, and 3, in the correct dependency order the Blueprint itself specifies (§6: each chapter's stated dependency has been honored before its topic was opened). Together they now cover: **who** exists in humanitarian work (actors), **what** those actors want and where that structurally conflicts (tensions), and **how** a case moves through time (lifecycle). This is a coherent starting spine — three foundational, load-bearing dimensions rather than three arbitrary topics.

**What remains entirely untouched**, relative to the BMP Blueprint's own nine-chapter structure:
- Chapter 4 (Business Capabilities), Chapter 5 (Business Services & Value Streams), Chapter 6 (Intervention Categories — already flagged, before this discovery phase began, as "the most urgent open research topic" in the BMP Blueprint's own §7), Chapter 7 (Operational Objectives & Success), Chapter 8 (Governance & Authority Boundaries), Chapter 9 (Boundary Conditions & Regional/Cultural Variation).
- Within the three completed topics, two structural gaps recur across all of them rather than belonging to any one: **individual operational-role granularity** (Registrant, Proxy, Field Verifier, Case Manager — asserted internally, never externally validated) and **deployment geography** (no dossier has evidence checked against a stated region, because no document anywhere in this project states one).
- TD-03 additionally surfaced, but did not investigate, the **programme-level (coordination/resourcing) lifecycle** as its own subject — it was identified as distinct from the case-level lifecycle but not explored on its own terms.

## 2. Emerging Concepts

Concepts that recur across more than one dossier, without being formalized into anything ontology-shaped here:

- **Accountability.** The single most cross-cutting concept so far — central to TD-02 (donor vs. beneficiary accountability is the strongest-evidenced tension found), present in TD-01 (Donor's actor status is itself an accountability-scope question), and implicated in TD-03 (outcome/impact measurement is an accountability-adjacent activity, and its placement is exactly what AR-006 leaves open).
- **Verification / claims vs. facts (epistemic humility).** Present in TD-01 (Field Verifier as a named role), central to TD-02 (BD-TD02-002, speed vs. verification), and structurally present in TD-03 (verification as a lifecycle stage; case-management "assessment" stage).
- **Formal/institutional vs. informal/community structures.** TD-01's Local Organisation and Islamic-giving vertical/horizontal split, TD-02's localization-vs-standardization tension. Every time this axis has been examined, external evidence confirmed it as sector-real rather than a Khidmat invention.
- **Organisation / Programme / Case.** All three dossiers touch this cluster from a different angle — TD-01 (the still-open Organisation/Programme contradiction), TD-03 (Programme as the unit of the coordination-level cycle; Case as the unit of the individual-level cycle).
- **Human oversight, consent, and dignity.** TD-02 (consent vs. necessity, automation vs. oversight), TD-03 (eligibility as an irreducibly human decision, TC3-3).
- **Confidence / uncertainty as subject matter, not just method.** All three dossiers' own Findings are confidence-rated by discovery method, but the *content* of what's being discovered increasingly is also about confidence — TD-02's speed/verification and consent/necessity tensions, and TD-01's claims/verification split, are all, at bottom, about how confident an organization is entitled to be before acting.

## 3. Emerging Patterns

Described only — none of the following is being modeled or formalized here:

- **A recurring dual-track / non-conflatable-pair pattern.** TD-03 found two explicit instances internally (case-cycle vs. programme-cycle; Engagement Stage vs. Human Development Stage), and TD-01's Donor contradiction shows a structurally similar shape (V1-delivery-scope-excluded vs. conceptually-included — two true things about the same actor that must not be collapsed into one). This has now appeared independently in two different dossiers, which is enough repetition to name as a pattern worth watching for, not yet enough to generalize as a law.
- **An altitude split between organizational-level and individual-level description.** TD-01 found sector coordination architecture operates at the organization level while Khidmat's own operational-role vocabulary operates at the individual level, with no evidence bridging them. TD-03 found the same split independently (programme cycle vs. case cycle). Two independent topics surfacing the same structural seam is a stronger signal than either alone.
- **Accountability keeps splitting in more than one direction whenever it is examined closely.** Every time a dossier has looked directly at accountability, it has found at least two legitimate, competing accountability relationships (donor vs. beneficiary in TD-02; organizational-intent vs. sector-reality in TD-01's Donor question), never a single clean line.
- **So far, internal pre-discovery artifacts have held up better against external evidence than they have been contradicted by it.** Across all three dossiers, external Tier B/D evidence corroborated an existing internal principle four separate times (TD-02's speed/verification and consent/necessity findings; TD-03's accountability-loop and Engagement/Development-Stage findings) and challenged an internal assumption only once (TD-03's outcome/impact placement question). This is a pattern about the discovery process's results, not about humanitarian reality itself, and is worth naming plainly rather than left as a scattered impression.
- **No external source has yet contradicted another external source.** Every disagreement found so far (CL-001, CL-002) is between two of Khidmat's own internal, pre-discovery artifacts — never between two independent sector sources. This bears directly on §5, below.

## 4. Assumptions

The Assumption Register currently holds six entries (AR-001 through AR-006).

**Assumptions appearing repeatedly, in shape if not in exact wording:** AR-001 (TD-01, operational-role vocabulary), AR-003 (TD-02, non-exhaustive actor-pair coverage), and AR-005 (TD-03, programme/case-cycle coupling) all trace back to the same root cause — **Tier A has not been executed once, in any of the three completed dossiers.** This is the single most repeated assumption-shape in the register, and it is a structural fact about this discovery process, not a per-topic coincidence.

AR-002 (general/cross-context applicability scope, no deployment geography stated) is the second recurring pattern: it was logged once, under TD-01, but its own text already states it applies to TD-02 and TD-03's Findings as well. It is functioning, in practice, as a project-wide standing assumption carried inside a per-topic register entry, rather than as three separate topic-scoped assumptions — worth naming as a structural observation about how the register is being used, not a defect in any individual entry.

**Assumptions most likely to require validation before downstream work should rely on them:** AR-001 is the highest-stakes of the register, since Khidmat's entire internal operational-role vocabulary (Registrant, Proxy, Field Verifier, Case Manager) rests on it and nothing external has yet checked it. AR-006 (TD-03, outcome/impact placement) is close behind — it will directly shape how a future Chapter 4 (Business Capabilities) topic gets scoped, since capabilities are typically organized around lifecycle stages, and TD-03 explicitly left that boundary unresolved.

**Assumptions most likely to affect downstream phases broadly rather than one chapter:** AR-002 (geography) is the clearest case — every dossier's Applicability Scope has been "general" by default, not by validated design, and this will silently propagate into every future BMP chapter unless it is addressed as its own decision rather than continuing to accumulate as a per-topic footnote.

No assumption is resolved or removed here; all six remain open exactly as logged.

## 5. Contradictions

The Contradiction Log currently holds two entries (CL-001, CL-002), both logged during TD-01's Tier C pass, both still open, neither touched by TD-02 or TD-03 (both dossiers explicitly checked and found no new contradiction).

**Are they isolated, or do they reveal broader uncertainty?** A specific, checkable observation: both entries trace to the same underlying cause — two internal, pre-discovery artifacts (`KHIDMAT_BUSINESS_LOGIC_BLUEPRINT_V1.md` and the archived `docs/90-reports/` ontology material) that were authored at different times without a reconciliation pass, not to any disagreement about humanitarian reality itself. Per §3's pattern above, no external Tier B/D source has ever contradicted another external source across three topics of collection. This suggests the uncertainty currently on record is narrower than "the project doesn't know what's true of the world" — it is closer to "the project has two un-reconciled prior drafts of its own thinking." That is a real and worth-tracking form of uncertainty, but a different, more tractable kind than a genuine disagreement about humanitarian practice would be.

**Should a future discovery task investigate them explicitly?** Not as a dedicated task — neither entry is a question further evidence-gathering can resolve; both require a Human Owner decision about which internal artifact's framing to keep, which is a governance action, not a discovery one. What *should* happen going forward: future dossiers (starting with TD-04) should continue actively watching for whether the same Programme/Organisation ambiguity resurfaces — it plausibly will, especially once Chapter 4 (Capabilities) or Chapter 8 (Governance & Authority Boundaries) are reached, since both are naturally organized around exactly this distinction.

## 6. Discovery Blueprint Review

Observations only — the Business Discovery Blueprint is not modified by this review.

- The Blueprint's core mechanics (tiers, the four-state epistemology, Topic Dossier structure, Evidence Freshness, Applicability Scope, the confidence-outcome completion criterion) have held up across three topics without needing to be forced or reinterpreted. No friction has yet surfaced between the Blueprint as written and the work of actually executing it.
- **One structural gap worth naming:** the Blueprint has no defined mechanism for a per-topic assumption to be "promoted" to a project-wide standing assumption once it recurs across dossiers — in practice, TD-02 and TD-03 have handled this informally and consistently (by explicitly stating "AR-002 applies here too" rather than duplicating the entry), but the Blueprint itself doesn't codify when or how that promotion should happen, or what it should mean for how future dossiers scope their own Applicability Scope by default.
- **A related gap:** Tier A's infeasibility is, in practice, now a standing fact about this discovery process as a whole, re-confirmed by reference in each dossier rather than re-derived — which is working well, but is also an informal convention the Blueprint doesn't explicitly describe as a valid pattern (its lifecycle, and this session's own instructions, treat "assess Tier A feasibility" as a per-topic step).
- **No planned task appears redundant.** TD-01, TD-02, and TD-03 have distinct, non-overlapping subject matter with productive (not circular) cross-referencing.
- **No task appears obviously missing relative to the Blueprint's own inherited BMP Blueprint chapter structure** — the sequence so far has followed that structure exactly.
- **One missed opportunity, minor:** TD-03 could have drawn more directly on TD-02's named tensions when describing where in the lifecycle those tensions actually surface (e.g., at which stage does the donor-accountability tension actually bite) — it did not do this, which is not a defect, but is a cross-linkage that a future review could ask TD-04 onward to do more deliberately.
- **Important humanitarian realities not yet represented in any completed dossier:** the intervention/assistance-type catalogue (Chapter 6) — already flagged as the single most urgent gap before this discovery phase even began — remains completely untouched; and despite TD-01 surfacing directly relevant material (Islamic-giving structures, informal local organizations), no dossier has yet asked "what region(s) or cultural context(s) is this project actually targeting" as a topic in its own right, even though its absence has now been logged as a recurring limitation three times.

**Overall:** Business Discovery remains on track as a *process* — rigorous, consistent, and evidence-first, exactly as the Blueprint requires. This review does not authorize proceeding to the Business Master Plan, and does not resolve any open Assumption or Contradiction. The clearest actionable signal from this checkpoint is not about TD-04's content — it is that the growing backlog of Human-Owner-pending governance decisions (CL-001, CL-002, and eventually a decision on AR-002's geography question) should be surfaced for resolution soon, in parallel with continuing discovery, rather than left to accumulate silently alongside it.
