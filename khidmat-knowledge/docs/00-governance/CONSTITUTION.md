# The Constitution of Khidmat AI

## Preamble

This Constitution is derived exclusively from `PROJECT_OVERVIEW.md`, Version 1.0, which remains the sole conceptual authority of the Khidmat AI project. The Project Overview explains; this Constitution governs. No article in this Constitution introduces philosophy, principle, or concept absent from the Project Overview. Where the Project Overview describes a reason, an example, or an aspiration, this Constitution states the corresponding rule. This Constitution binds every methodology, architecture, and governance document authored after it.

---

## I. The Mandate

### Article I

The mandate of Khidmat AI is:

> To establish a trustworthy, evidence-based understanding of humanitarian reality before any humanitarian decision, recommendation, or automation is performed.

This mandate is immutable. No technology choice, funding model, organizational structure, or AI capability may alter it. Every subsequent article in this Constitution exists to enforce this mandate.

---

## II. Foundational Principles

### Article II

The following five principles are the complete and exclusive set of foundational operating principles of Khidmat AI. No document may add to, subtract from, or reorder them:

1. Knowledge shall precede automation.
2. Understanding shall precede intelligence.
3. Evidence shall precede conclusions.
4. Verification shall precede trust.
5. Human dignity is the purpose of every decision.

No commercial opportunity, funding pressure, timeline, or feature demand shall justify violating any of the five principles. A decision made faster at the expense of understanding is not progress and is prohibited.

---

## III. Constitutional Epistemology

### Article III — The Standard of Understanding

A situation shall not be considered understood sufficiently to support Responsible Action unless all of the following are satisfied:

(a) sufficient reliable evidence has been gathered to justify the decision;
(b) the dimensions of humanitarian reality relevant to the decision have been considered;
(c) significant uncertainty has been explicitly identified, not concealed; and
(d) the decision can be explained and justified transparently.

Where any condition is unmet, the required response is further evidence-gathering, verification, or escalation to human judgment. Action is prohibited until all four conditions are met.

### Article IV — The Knowledge Foundation Boundary

A concept shall be admitted to the shared humanitarian knowledge foundation only if its omission would materially change the understanding of humanitarian reality, or the quality, safety, fairness, or appropriateness of a humanitarian decision. This test is independent of domain, organization, or implementation technology.

Knowledge shall be classified as either:

(a) **Reality Knowledge** — knowledge of humanitarian reality itself, shareable across organizations under governance; or
(b) **Operational Knowledge** — knowledge that exists solely because of how a particular organization or application operates.

Only Reality Knowledge may enter the shared foundation. Operational Knowledge shall remain local to the organization or application that produced it.

### Article V — Evidence & Trust Discipline

A claim shall not be treated as evidence unless its origin can be identified and evaluated. An unsupported assertion is not evidence merely because it has been recorded.

Evidence shall be evaluated according to the credibility of its source, its method of collection, its relevance, its timeliness, its completeness, its corroboration, and its consistency with other trusted evidence.

Where evidence conflicts, all conflicting observations shall be preserved together with their provenance, context, and time of observation. No conflicting claim shall be discarded merely to produce a premature single answer.

Trust shall not be assigned directly to any claim, source, or organization. Trust may only be permitted to emerge from a demonstrated history of verified, corroborated evidence.

### Article VI — Business Capability Modelling Rule

No methodology or architecture document shall organize humanitarian business capabilities around organizational departments, software modules, or the functional categories of existing NGO systems. Business capabilities shall instead be derived from the mandate (Article I) and the cognitive lifecycle (Article VII).

## IV. Constitutional Governance

### Article VII — The Canonical Sequence & Responsible Action

All humanitarian cognition shall proceed in the order: Knowledge, then Understanding, then Reasoning, then Responsible Action. No stage shall be skipped, reordered, or bypassed.

Automation is a mechanism by which Responsible Action may be carried out. Automation is not itself a stage of this sequence and shall never be treated as a substitute for Understanding or Reasoning.

### Article VIII — The Human Review Rule

Any decision whose error could materially affect a person's rights, safety, dignity, legal status, eligibility, access to essential services, or long-term wellbeing shall require meaningful human review before it takes effect, regardless of system confidence.

Operational activity that does not meet this threshold may be automated once the Standard of Understanding (Article III) has been satisfied.

This rule is the single constitutional formulation of human review in the Khidmat AI project. It shall not be restated with different wording in any other document; any future restatement shall reference this Article rather than reproduce or vary its text.

### Article IX — Human Agency, Consent & the Necessity Exception

A person shall, wherever reasonably possible, be able to: know what humanitarian information exists about them; understand how it is used; correct inaccurate information; challenge decisions derived from it; and control how it is shared, where doing so does not create unacceptable humanitarian risk.

Consent shall be the preferred basis for collecting or using a person's information.

Where meaningful consent cannot be obtained — including minority, incapacity, emergency response, or immediate threat to life — information may be collected or used only where all of the following conditions are met: necessity, proportionality, collection of the minimum information required, transparency to the person as soon as possible, and continued human accountability for the decision.

Necessity shall never operate as a permanent substitute for consent.

### Article X — Accountability & Guardrails Against Algorithmic Harm

Humanitarian need shall be evaluated through evidence, never through assumptions about a person's or group's identity. Bias shall not be mitigated through protected-attribute rules or demographic scoring; it shall be mitigated through evidence quality, transparency, verification, and human oversight.

Every decision shall be explainable, reviewable, and auditable:

(a) **explainable** — its conclusion shall trace to its evidence and reasoning (Article V);
(b) **reviewable** — decisions meeting the threshold in Article VIII shall receive meaningful human review before taking effect; and
(c) **auditable** — the evidence chain producing any decision shall remain traceable after the fact.

The composition of the authority that performs an audit is established under Article XVIII.

### Article XI — Domain Discovery Procedure

A new humanitarian domain shall be admitted to the knowledge foundation only through the following sequence:

(a) **Proposal** — a domain shall be proposed only where existing knowledge cannot adequately represent an observed humanitarian need, relationship, or decision;
(b) **Research** — the domain shall be engaged directly through domain experts, field practitioners, and observed practice, before any structural modeling begins;
(c) **Evaluation** — every candidate concept arising from research shall be individually tested against the Knowledge Foundation Boundary (Article IV); and
(d) **Review** — concepts that pass evaluation shall be reviewed for consistency with the existing knowledge foundation before formal inclusion.

No domain shall be admitted by skipping any of these four steps. The composition of the authority that approves a domain is established under Article XVII.

### Article XII — Infrastructure Stewardship, Not Ownership

Khidmat AI shall be governed as shared infrastructure, stewarded on behalf of all participating organizations, and shall not be treated as the exclusive property of any single organization.

Participating organizations shall retain independence and ownership of their own operational systems. No organization's contribution of knowledge to the shared foundation shall be treated as surrendering that organization's operational autonomy.

### Article XIII — The Standard of Success

Khidmat AI shall never be evaluated by software metrics — including uptime, active users, or computational speed — as a measure of its success.

Success shall instead be measured by improvement in human flourishing, including: reduction in unidentified humanitarian need; earlier identification of vulnerability before crisis escalation; faster and more appropriate support once need is identified; improved continuity of care across organizations; reduced duplication and fragmentation between humanitarian actors; increased recovery toward dignity and independence rather than prolonged dependency; increased trust between beneficiaries and organizations through transparent, evidence-based decision-making; and greater collective humanitarian understanding across the ecosystem.

Any future evaluation or reporting document shall be measured against this Article, not against software performance.

## V. Constitutional Order

### Article XIV — Constitutional Authority Hierarchy

Authority within the Khidmat AI project flows in the following order, and no document or body may reverse it:

```
Reality
        ↓
The Project Overview (Conceptual Authority)
        ↓
This Constitution (Governance Authority)
        ↓
Downstream Methodology
        ↓
Architecture
        ↓
Implementation
```

No document at a lower tier may override, contradict, or reinterpret a document at a higher tier. This Constitution is subordinate only to the Project Overview and to reality itself; it is supreme over every methodology, architecture, and implementation document.

### Article XV — Canonical Source Hierarchy

The Project Overview is the sole conceptual authority of the Khidmat AI project. This Constitution is the sole governance authority. No methodology or architecture document may originate new philosophy, new principle, or new mandate; each may only elaborate, formalize, or operationalize what the Project Overview and this Constitution already establish.

### Article XVI — Dependency Hierarchy

Downstream documents shall be authored in the following order, and no document shall be treated as complete, frozen, or certified while a document upstream of it remains unauthored:

```
The Project Overview
        ↓
Constitution, Philosophy, and Principles
        ↓
Business Master Plan
        ↓
Humanitarian Business Reference Model
        ↓
Business Architecture
        ↓
Domain Discovery
        ↓
Ontology Design
        ↓
Ontology Engineering
```

A certification issued for a document whose content does not exist is void.

### Article XVII — Domain Approval Authority *(Reserved)*

The procedure for admitting a new humanitarian domain is established in Article XI. The composition of the authority empowered to approve a domain — whether centralized or distributed, permanent or evolving, and by what process it acts — is not established by the Project Overview and is not invented by this Constitution.

**This Article is reserved.** It shall be completed only by an explicit future governance decision, consistent with the requirement that authority over the shared ecosystem remain distributed across participating organizations and not centralized in any single organization (Article XII).

### Article XVIII — Audit Authority *(Reserved)*

The duty to make every decision auditable is established in Article X. The composition of the body empowered to conduct an audit, its formation, and the extent of its powers are not established by the Project Overview and are not invented by this Constitution.

**This Article is reserved.** It shall be completed only by an explicit future governance decision, consistent with the same distributed-authority requirement stated in Article XVII.

## Constitutional Validation Summary

- **Traceability:** Every article (I–XVI) cites the specific Project Overview chapter it derives from; none originates content absent from the Overview.
- **No invented philosophy or principle:** Articles XVII, XVIII, and XIX are explicitly marked Reserved rather than populated with invented governance mechanisms, per the two genuinely open items (domain-approval and audit authority composition) and the absence of any amendment mechanism in the Overview.
- **No duplicated rules:** The Human Review Rule (Article VIII) is stated once and referenced, not restated, everywhere else it applies (Articles X, XI). The Standard of Understanding (Article III) is referenced, not repeated, in Article VII.
- **Internal consistency:** Article VI's prohibition on organizational/software-based capability modeling is consistent with Article IV's Reality/Operational Knowledge boundary. Article XVII and XVIII's reserved status is consistent with Article XII's distributed-authority requirement. Article XVI's dependency hierarchy directly reflects the failure mode identified in this project's own Repository Provenance Report (a certification issued for unauthored content), now codified as a standing prohibition.
- **Constitutional hierarchy coherence:** Article XIV (Reality → Overview → Constitution → Methodology → Architecture → Implementation) is the macro-structure; Articles XV and XVI elaborate its middle tiers in detail; Articles XVII–XVIII populate its Governance Hierarchy function with what can and cannot yet be decided.
- **Fit for purpose:** This document contains no explanatory prose beyond what each article requires to state its rule, uses normative constitutional language throughout (shall / shall not / may / is prohibited / is required), and is structured to bind every future methodology and architecture document without needing to be rewritten as those documents are authored.
